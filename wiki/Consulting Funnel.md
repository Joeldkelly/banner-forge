# Consulting Funnel

banner-forge is free. Consulting is the business. This note exists because every design decision — README structure, landing page CTA, video outro — should be evaluated against *"does this convert skill users into consulting inquiries?"*

## The model

```
skill discovered (HN, X, newsletter, YouTube, awesome-list)
     │
     ▼
GitHub repo visit → README → landing page (joel.design/banner-forge)
     │
     ▼
"Need a custom skill for your agency?" CTA
     │
     ▼
Calendly: 30-minute intro call
     │
     ▼
Scoped engagement: $X to build a custom Claude skill
```

## Why this, not product revenue

### The ad-creative SaaS market is already claimed

Pencil charges $240+/year. AdCreative.ai, Bannerify, Celtra — all funded. Competing on product features means:

- Fighting pricing wars with funded teams.
- Building a support org.
- Continuous feature parity pressure.

None of this plays to a solo independent's strengths.

### Custom Claude skills are unclaimed

As of early 2026, almost no agency or internal marketing team has:

- A dedicated internal Claude skill for their specific workflow.
- Someone on staff who builds them.
- A pattern for integrating Claude skills into existing creative tooling.

That's a services market. Services play to a solo's strengths:

- High hourly value.
- Relationship-driven sales (one retweet from Pietro Schirano reaches 50 buyers).
- Portfolio effect — each engagement becomes a case study.

### The skill is the portfolio piece

A published, widely-starred, open-source skill is worth more than a traditional agency portfolio. It:

- Demonstrates capability without a client permissions conversation.
- Has public metrics (stars, forks, installs).
- Generates its own inbound lead flow.
- Compounds — as the ecosystem grows, the repo becomes more discoverable.

### Pieter Levels / Pietro Schirano playbook

Both run every product under their personal name because buyers of freelance consulting search for **people**, not tools.

- Pieter Levels drives $250K monthly revenue across multiple small products — all @levelsio.
- Pietro Schirano stacks Claude Engineer, DesignerGPT, MagicPath under @skirano.

Separate product brands only make sense with a team and funding. Joel is one person.

## What the consulting offer looks like

### Packaged offer (target by Week 12)

**"Custom Claude skill for your agency"** — flat-rate, scoped, delivered in 2-4 weeks.

Typical scopes:

- **Internal brand pipeline** — an agency's specific brand-book → every deliverable pattern.
- **Campaign-QA skill** — ingests final deliverables, checks against brand guidelines, flags problems.
- **Figma-to-email** — the obvious sibling skill.
- **Research-to-brief** — competitive intelligence skill feeding creative brief generation.

### Pricing heuristic

Based on the research report's positioning: "replace a full day of banner production" frames the value. A single campaign day saved is worth ~$2,000 to an agency. A skill that saves that day on every campaign is worth 6 figures annually in productive capacity.

Pricing anchor: $15K–$40K per skill engagement. Pre-sold against the value, not the hours.

### Discovery call flow

30 minutes. Three questions:

1. **What's the repeating workflow you're losing time to?**
2. **Who on your team would use the skill, and how often?**
3. **What does "done" look like — a working skill, a trained team, a maintained skill?**

The goal isn't to sell on the call. It's to surface whether there's a real engagement. A high-quality "not yet" is better than a desperate "yes."

## Conversion decisions this forces

### README ordering

Put **the install one-liner** first (reduces friction to try it), the **consulting CTA** as a sincere line at the bottom ("I help agencies build custom skills — book a call"), not a banner ad.

### Landing page hierarchy

See [[Landing Page]]. Hero → how it works → proof → consulting. The consulting block is *visible* but not the first thing. Earn attention with the product, then make the ask.

### Video outro

Every launch video (primary 45-60s, YouTube long-form) ends with 5 seconds of "I'm Joel, I build custom Claude skills for agencies — link in description." Not a hard sell. Just presence.

### Newsletter

One email list. "Future skills and agency case studies." Low-frequency. The newsletter sells future skills, which seed future consulting conversations.

## Metrics to watch

- **GitHub stars** — leading indicator of reach.
- **Landing page → Calendly clicks** — the actual conversion metric.
- **Discovery calls booked / month** — what matters.
- **Discovery → engagement rate** — qualifier efficiency.

Do not optimize for installs in isolation. Installs are vanity unless they produce discovery calls.

## What to stop doing if it's not working

Kill criteria from [[Roadmap]]:

- Day 60, <100 stars, <10 installs, zero discovery calls → revisit positioning.
- Day 90, fewer than 3 discovery calls → the consulting pitch isn't landing. Try a vertical cut (retail agencies, SaaS in-house teams) rather than horizontal "any agency."
