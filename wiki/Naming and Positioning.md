# Naming and Positioning

## The name: banner-forge

Primary pick. Rationale:

- **Descriptive.** You know what it does without a tagline.
- **Hyphen-safe** for the SKILL.md regex (`^[a-z0-9]+(-[a-z0-9]+)*$`).
- **No conflicting USPTO marks** in software class 9 or advertising class 35 (checked against TESS).
- **`-forge` suffix** reads as "builder tool" to a technical audience. Precedent: Laravel Forge, Neo-Forge, SvelteKit. Never sounds AI-generic.

### Alternatives if `banner-forge` is taken on a surface that matters

1. **`adloom`** — invented word, strongest legal defensibility, brandable.
2. **`bannerwright`** — craft framing, matches designer identity.
3. `multiply` — value-prop framing, too broad.
4. `impression` — ad-term pun, slight risk of common-word trademarks.

### Names to never use

- Anything with "Pencil" in it.
- "Open X" where X is a live commercial mark (OpenAI disputes, OpenTofu vs. Terraform).
- "Ad-" prefixes that overlap with Adobe, AdRoll, AdCreative.ai registrations.

See [[Trademark]].

## Taglines

### Primary (recommended default)

> **One Figma frame. Every banner size. Zero manual exports.**

Nine words. Names input, output, and removed pain. Used on the landing page, README, X thread opener.

### Alternates

- "Open-source banner production for the agent era."
- "The Claude skill that ships the campaign while you sleep."

Second works in technical communities (HN, Claude Code Discord). Third works in agency-creator circles where "ship while you sleep" reads as aspirational, not cynical.

## The narrative anchor

> **Replace the resizing day.**

Not "replace Pencil."

Pencil is the competitor. Pencil is not the pain. The pain every designer feels is the 6-to-40-hour resizing grind on every campaign (Santa Cruz Software survey: 85% struggle, 52% spend 30+ hrs/week).

Anchor every piece of copy to *the lost day*. Pencil comes up only as a named comparison in the README and landing page under nominative fair use — never as the hero.

## Why this positioning works

- **Names the profession** — "senior designer" in the opening tweet. Credibility before persuasion.
- **Gives a specific number** — "40 banner sizes in 45 seconds." Quantifies the compression.
- **Reveals the moat** — "this is what I used to charge clients a full day for." Both humble-brag and price anchor.
- **Anchors on free** — removes the trial / budget-approval barrier.

## The hook (primary launch tweet)

> *"I'm a senior designer. I built a Claude skill that generates 40 IAB banner sizes from one Figma frame in 45 seconds. Free. Open source. This is what I used to charge clients a full day for. github.com/joeldkelly/banner-forge"*

Works because it names the profession, gives a specific number, compresses time, reveals the moat, and anchors on free — in one tweet.

**Avoid:** "I built it in a weekend with Claude Code." That framing is saturated in 2026. Only use it if paired with a real agency case study (ex-Dyson campaign run, etc.).
